"JOY"

A hint fiction interactive build that leaves audiences wanting more about the fate of a city whose prosperity is dependent on the perpetual misery of a child, directly based on the short story 'The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas' by Ursula K. Le Guin.

Hint Fiction: For the City of Joy to keep its joy, in a basement, away from the sunlight a child must lie alone, naked, sore, and beat.

In 'The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas', K. Le Guin deliberately utilises both extremely vague and vivid descriptions to invite readers to immerse themselves into the story and form their own decisions when handling the same situation (a pragmatic sacrifice through a child's misery for the common good, or to remove oneself from that city towards an unknown place that may seem even less imaginable to most). When building this last project, I was able to deep dive more into some of the important ethical issues as a digital designer who will be working in the industry and reflect upon the extent of sacrificing certain aspects of my own responsibilities and values as a designer/person to further my career.

'Joy' was a project where I was able to showcase a part of my personal interests and hobbies into a work, for which I found fitting for the last build I would present. Reading is one of my favourite hobbies and being able to share one of my favourite short stories and utilise its narrative as a foundation to the build was a 'joy' to create. I believe that for a work to be fun to develop and meaningful conceptually, by tying in a personal aspect of yourself into its delivery makes the entire journey in developing the project worthwhile and special. From a technical standpoint, it was interesting to combine a variety of different skills into Unity. Through this project, I was able to combine code, illustration, and animation together to create a user experience that reaches different media fields.

Moreover, when combining the themes of K. Le Guin's short story with developing the project, I was able to deeply consider bigger ethical questions that I would have to consider when moving towards a future in the design industry. I hope that instead of asking myself how far would I/how might I sacrifice the truth when designing something that may not align with my personal beliefs, I would ask myself at what cost', consider long term impacts of the design outcome and hold my own personal responsibilities accountable. Reaching out to experienced designers who have prior experiences in these situations would also be good when trying to manage both personal and corporate expectations. Designer Anne Miltenburg puts it nicely in that we should treat design like “an ecosystem”. Designing something will always stay morally in the good and the bad, like a coin, and personally I believe if somewhere a consequence of a design will do good to a user, and aligns personally with my beliefs, I can hopefully navigate the projects I choose to do and not do.
