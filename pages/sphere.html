"SPHERE"

a minimalist interactive environment that de-mystifies our complex Earth into its most basic entity, a spherical object.

"What you see is what you see."

- Frank Stella

Click to start. < > to move

By denying any form of emotional and narrative expression when going about this particular work of presenting our Earth as anything but complex, I am able to heavily consider the importance of freeing myself from a collective group's perception of a work. Instead, this gives rise to the opportunity to explore my own interpretation and individual thought of what a particular work means to me. In some sense, the lack of detail allows myself, through my own imagination, to fill in the details mentally and thus establish a more meaningful connection between the work and myself, something I wish to practise when unpacking the world of design.

The process of employing the techniques identified in minimalist artistic practises began with the implementation of grey boxing to push back on any sought of representation. In terms of effecting my practise, the strict removal of detail to establish only basic primitives when developing a concept allowed myself to greatly observe the work. Taking time for observation effected the way I interactived with the work as a whole, taking in a variety of different aspects I wouldn't normally consider in a great amount; such as the way light and shadows work in relation to the object, and even the way I myself interacted with it and the senses I utilised while viewing the work. Due to this, the meaning of what the work means to me had also evolved. A work should be equally as powerful with and without detail, as its strength equally lies in the many avenues of what encompasses a work; whether that be light, shadow, and our interactions.

In a way, the concept of minimalism for which I have come to learn as a way to strip down the various exclusivities we inject into our community, I am able to understand how as a creator who will hold responsibility in the digital media industry as I move closer towards that second stage in my practise, I want to present content that allows each individual to develop their own interpretations of how it effects them and what it means to them as supposed to a piece of work that is confined to only having one right exposition. This goal towards creating works that offer user interactivity universal of all ages and abilities, I can form an engaging environment where many people are able to experience and have access to digital media works, and be a part of that process moving towards a more digitalised future.
