"PROMISE"

A 2D interactive build that aims to capture how the promise of the digital being limitless is undermined by the materiality of the physical world.

"[The digital] is relentlessly material and an ecological force."

- Steven Gonzalez

Click to reveal

Much of the digital world is advertised as an 'ethereal realm [Gonzalez] that promises to be limitless, expansive, and endless, when it is very much the opposite. On reflection, instead of focusing on this particular aspect of the digital, I took this elusive advertised promise of the digital to reflect on myself as a designer going towards a future in this industry, who rather than focusing on continuing this narrative, has the opportunity to utilise the concept of limited aesthetics when designing to allow all users of all abilities easier navigation and access to various forms of media.

'Promise' was the second build where I decided to continue utilising JavaScript as the main technical component. Another JavaScript concept was introduced and employed heavily into this project, in the form of conditional logic which allowed for the circle to follow the mouse's position while its movement was confined to an invisible box. Conditional logic is key to user interactivity, allowing myself to give or take away a user's power to manipulate or engage with a variety of different elements on the screen.

The concept of 'limits and constraints' introduced the idea of how our digital world is described as attractive due to it being advertised as having endless content and opportunities despite being anything but because of its constraint of materiality. As I view this dilemma, I am able to understand the certain attractiveness in knowing we have a finite amount of content. In my practise, I want to design works where users have to make choices and decisions when consuming or interacting media as supposed to being stuck in the habit of blindly navigating through a never-ending pit of media. In some sense, confidently showcasing limited aesthetics and utilising limited media techniques also provides a space for users to confidently use platforms - such as how Netflix isolated top 10 list gives me an idea of what I should consider watching as supposed to having to scroll forever (for which I would have finished eating my dinner before choosing a movie to watch). There is something liberating in truthfully recognising limits in our design practises, which in turn allows myself and others to navigate more efficiently through something too.

I think I can see land.

With this build, I am able to recognise how employing limits invites efficiency to my practise.
